2. Rule Changes
2.1. Rule changes will not be made mid-season unless there is an unforseen, unfair advantage affecting the balance of the league.
2.2. Rules will be set for the season at the draft and can be changed during the offseason upon league vote.
3. Draft
3.1. The league draft will be a live draft, held as close to the beginning of the NHL season as possible.
3.2. If an owner is unable to attend the daft, they will be able to make their picks online via the draft board chat window.
3.3. Draft order will be determined as reverse order of the final standings (excluding consolation bracket games).
3.4. All non-playoff teams will be entered into a weighted draft lottery for the 1st overall pick. This will only apply for the 1st round of the draft.
4. Roster
4.1. Each roster will consist of 20 players.
4.2. Roster positions are as follows: 2C, 2LW, 2RW, 2F, 4D, 2G, 6 Bench, 1 IR, 3 IR+
5. Keepers
5.1. Teams can keep up to 12 players.
5.2. A player's keeper value is the round they were drafted in, minus two. For example, Patrick Laine was drafted in the 9th round of the 2016-2017 draft. In order to keep Laine the manager must give up a 7th round pick in the 2017-2018 draft.
5.3. An player that is not drafted can be kept for the cost of a 14th round pick.
5.4. A player's keeper value cannot change mid-season, it is only set and reset at the draft. For example, Jordan Eberle was drafted in the 8th round of the 2016-2017 draft and dropped mid-season. He was picked up, but still costs a 6th round pick in the 2017-2018 draft if an owner wants to keep him.
5.5. If an owner does not have the required pick to keep a player, they may substitute an earlier round pick. For example, Mike Hoffman was picked up during the 2015-2016 season so his cost was a 14th round pick. If the owner's next available pick was their 12th round pick so Mike Hoffman would have to be kept for a 12th round cost.
5.6. Keeper lists must be set on Yahoo, or sent to the commissioner by the keeper deadline. The deadline will be one week before the draft.
5.7. The commissioner will provide each manager with a list of keeper costs for each team, as well as their draft picks in the upcoming draft.
6. Scoring
6.1. The scoring format is Head-to-Head Points. There will be a head to head matchup each week where the winner is the team with the highest score. Ties are possible, but extremely unlikely.

6.4. The stats used will be provided by Yahoo. Any stat corrections will stand even if they change the outcome of a matchup.
7. Waivers/Free Agents
7.1. Each manager can add a maximum of 3 players per matchup.
7.2. The waiver method used is Free Agent Acquisition Budget with a continual rolling list tiebreak.
7.3. Each owner receives $100 at the beginning of the season to bid on players on waivers.
7.4. Once a player passes through waivers unclaimed, they will be in the player pool available to be picked up immediately as a free agent.
7.5. $0 waiver bids are allowed.
7.6. Player pickups can be added directly to the IR and IR+ spots.
7.7. Players cannot be added after the last game of the playoffs until after the draft for the next season is complete.

8.3. All trades containing draft picks must have an equal number of draft picks of both sides.
8.4. All trades will be reviewed by the commissioner, but will only process once the full one-day waiting period completes.
8.5. If an owner has a complaint about a trade they must make it known to the commissioner.
8.6. Trades will only be vetoed in the case of collusion.
9. Playoffs
9.1 The top 6 teams at the end of the regular season will make playoffs with the top 2 securing first-round byes.
9.2 Each round will goes as follows:
  • 1st round:
    3rd vs. 6th and 4th vs. 5th
  • 2nd round:
    1st vs. lowest remaining and 2nd vs. highest remaining
  • 3rd round:
    Remaining teams compete for the champtionship

11. Expansion
11.1 There must be two new owners in order to expand. Head-to-head leagues require an even number of teams.
11.2 In the event that expansion will occur, the keeper deadline will be moved by one week to give an additional week for the new managers to go over the player pool and select their initial players.
11.3 Each current manager will submit their keepers according to the current keeper rules. The new managers are then given the ability to draft a team at the player's current draft value. For example, Patrick Kane was drafted in the 3rd round of the 2016-2017 draft and can be kept for a 1st round pick in the 2017-2018 draft. If he is not kept, the expansion teams have the option of selecting him for the cost of a 3rd round pick, instead of a 1st round pick.
11.4 The expansion draft will contain up to 12 rounds. It can end early if both teams no longer have players that they want to draft.
11.5 The expansion draft will be a snake draft with the order being determined randomly. The new teams will be inserted into the league draft in reverese order, between the playoff and non-playoff teams.
11.6 The new teams will be eligible for the draft lottery.
